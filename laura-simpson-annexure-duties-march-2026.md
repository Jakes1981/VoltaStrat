# ANNEXURE "A"

## DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

### To the Letter of Employment between Volta Labs and Laura Simpson

**Position:** Director, Community Experience

**Reporting Line:** Chief Innovation Officer (CINO)

**Location:** Halifax, Nova Scotia

**Date:** March 2026

---

## 1. PURPOSE OF THE ROLE

1.1 The Director of Community Experience is responsible for the activation, retention, and overall experience of founders and builders across Volta Labs' community and programmes.

1.2 The role serves as the primary operational and strategic link between Volta's three interconnected pillars: Startup Success, Builders & Community, and the SME AI Lab.

1.3 The Employee shall be responsible for programme operations, community health, coaching coordination, and the builder pipeline that connects community talent to Lab deployment.

1.4 The title "Director" reflects scope of responsibility. The role requires direct, hands-on execution across all areas described herein.

---

## 2. CORE DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

### 2.1 Residency Programme Leadership

2.1.1 Manage and oversee the day-to-day operations of the Volta Residency programme.

2.1.2 Own and operate the full application and onboarding pipeline, including the processing of applications, coordination of interviews, communication of outcomes to applicants, and onboarding of approved residents.

2.1.3 Ensure that every new and existing resident is fully informed of the resources, tools, and support infrastructure available to them and understands how to access and utilise them.

2.1.4 Maintain data integrity within Impact OS (Volta's operational platform) to support coaching decisions, portfolio health reporting, and go/no-go evaluations.

2.1.5 Coordinate the monthly evaluation cycle in collaboration with the CINO.

### 2.2 Coach Coordination

2.2.1 Serve as the direct reporting line for Volta's external coaching bench.

2.2.2 Manage the coaching roster, establish and maintain briefing rhythms, and prepare portfolio context materials in advance of coaching cycles.

2.2.3 Conduct regular one-on-one check-ins with each coach to identify and surface issues at the earliest opportunity.

2.2.4 Maintain the coordination, reporting, and data layer that enables coaching quality oversight and coach development (the latter being led by the CINO).

### 2.3 Support Engagement and Invoice Oversight

2.3.1 Review all open support engagements and liaise with advisors and coaches to ensure that hours approved in Impact OS are consistent with hours actually logged.

2.3.2 Verify all submitted invoices against system records prior to forwarding for payment processing.

2.3.3 Flag and resolve any discrepancies between approved, logged, and invoiced hours before they escalate.

2.3.4 Process and approve AWS credit applications for portfolio companies and maintain corresponding records in Impact OS.

### 2.4 Monthly Evaluation Processing

2.4.1 Work with the CINO through the monthly evaluation cycle, reviewing which companies are to advance to one-on-one coaching support, which are to continue in their current track, and which are to exit the programme.

2.4.2 Manage the allocation of company teams to coaches for each subsequent month following evaluation decisions.

2.4.3 Ensure that all companies awaiting a coaching seat submit their required monthly updates prior to evaluation week (the last week of each calendar month).

2.4.4 Update Impact OS to reflect all evaluation outcomes and communicate decisions to the relevant founders and coaches.

### 2.5 Builders and Community Strategy

2.5.1 Own the health, growth, and experience of Volta's builder network across Atlantic Canada.

2.5.2 Develop and execute community strategy, experience architecture, and engagement programming.

2.5.3 Lead province activation efforts beyond Nova Scotia, including New Brunswick, Prince Edward Island, and Newfoundland and Labrador.

2.5.4 Establish and maintain relationships with community leaders across all active provinces and implement a regular check-in system to support distributed community health.

2.5.5 Manage the Pulse data programme, including response collection, analysis, and health synthesis.

2.5.6 Identify high-potential builders suitable for the co-founder pathway and facilitate their progression toward startup team placement.

### 2.6 Sprint Programme — Community Synthesis

2.6.1 Monitor and interpret builder demand and feedback patterns arising from the community.

2.6.2 Surface trends and recommendations to the Chief Executive Officer for Sprint direction and cohort planning decisions.

2.6.3 Track and document outcome data to assess Sprint's impact on the builder pipeline.

2.6.4 Ensure that community signals reach leadership in a form that is timely, accurate, and actionable.

### 2.7 Builder-to-Lab Pipeline

2.7.1 Serve as the operational bridge between the Builders & Community pillar and the SME AI Lab.

2.7.2 Conduct monthly synchronisation meetings with the Lab Director to align builder readiness with Lab engagement demand.

2.7.3 Own the supply side of the Lab's vetted, deployment-ready builder pipeline.

### 2.8 Community Data Quality and Hygiene

2.8.1 Maintain the accuracy, currency, and consistency of all community and portfolio data within Impact OS, including but not limited to Pulse scores, community engagement records, company milestones, and founder context.

2.8.2 Ensure that the CINO and Chief Executive Officer have access to real-time, accurate community and portfolio health data at all times without the need for separately produced reports.

2.8.3 Prepare and deliver regular community health reports and synthesis documents as required.

---

## 3. RECURRING OPERATIONAL DUTIES

In addition to the strategic responsibilities set out in Section 2, the Employee shall be responsible for the following recurring operational duties:

3.1 Processing of new Residency applications and coordination of applicant interviews.

3.2 Onboarding of new residents and ongoing orientation regarding available resources and systems.

3.3 Review and approval of AWS credit applications for portfolio companies.

3.4 Reconciliation of hours and invoices for coaches and advisors against Impact OS records.

3.5 Monitoring of company updates in Impact OS across all active residents, with particular attention to those receiving one-on-one coaching support.

3.6 Follow-up with companies awaiting coaching seats to ensure submission of monthly updates prior to evaluation week.

3.7 Processing of monthly evaluations and allocation of company teams to coaches for the following month.

---

## 4. KEY WORKING RELATIONSHIPS

4.1 **CINO (Direct Supervisor):** The Employee reports directly to the CINO and collaborates closely on monthly evaluations, coaching quality oversight, and strategic direction.

4.2 **Chief Executive Officer:** The Employee provides community health reports, programme data, and Sprint synthesis to the CEO. The Employee's data and reporting serve as evidence for external stakeholder communications.

4.3 **Director of Events and Operations (Peer):** Both roles report to the CINO. The Director of Events and Operations is responsible for event logistics and Sprint scheduling. The Employee provides strategic direction and scope clarity to support that execution.

4.4 **Lab Director (Peer):** The Employee conducts monthly pipeline coordination with the Lab Director. The Employee's community constitutes the Lab's primary talent source.

4.5 **Coaching Bench (Direct Reports):** The Employee serves as the reporting line for all external coaches and is responsible for their coordination, briefing, and operational support.

---

## 5. GENERAL

5.1 The duties and responsibilities set out in this Annexure are not exhaustive and may be amended from time to time by the Employer, in consultation with the Employee, to reflect the evolving needs of the organisation.

5.2 The Employee shall perform such additional duties as may reasonably be required and which are consistent with the nature and level of the position.

5.3 The Employee acknowledges that this is a hands-on role at a lean organisation and that, except where specific responsibilities are allocated to other team members as described herein, the Employee is expected to execute the work directly rather than delegate it.
