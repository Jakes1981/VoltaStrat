Role Definitions

Proposed job descriptions for the two community and programs roles as Volta enters Q2 2026. These reflect the strategic decisions from the March 2026 planning sessions and the org clarity work underway.

Role 01

Director of Community & Programs

Reports to: Jaco Burger, COO  ·  Full-time  ·  Halifax, NS

Purpose of this Role

Volta's three pillars — Startup Success, Builders & Community, and the SME AI Lab — only work at scale when someone owns the operational and relational layer that connects them. This role is that connective tissue. The Director of Community & Programs leads the day-to-day running of the Residency program, owns the Builders & Community strategy, and serves as the bridge between the community talent pool and the Lab's deployment needs.

This is a hands-on role at a small organisation. "Director" reflects the scope, not a separation from execution. Laura does the work — she runs the pipeline, coordinates the coaches, processes the evaluations, manages the community relationships, and oversees Sprint. The execution support she has is the coaching and advisor roster (for founder delivery) and Amy (for event and logistics execution). Everything else, she does. Volta's agent automation stack protects her time by handling the repetitive coordination layer — so the hours she has are spent on the work that actually requires human judgment and relationships.

Core Responsibilities
Startup Success — Program Leadership. Day-to-day running of the Residency program. Owns the application and onboarding pipeline (applications in, interviews, approvals, onboarding), ensures every resident knows what they have access to and how to use it, coordinates the evaluation cycle with Jaco each month, and maintains the data integrity in Impact OS that makes coaching and go/no-go decisions possible.
Coach Coordination. Owns the operational layer of the coaching bench — currently Jared Perry, Jaco, and Shri, with Shahriar Amin joining as the bench grows. Roster management, regular briefing rhythms, portfolio context prep before coaching cycles, and 1:1 check-ins to surface issues early. Jaco coaches founders and coaches the coaches — he holds quality standards and develops the bench. This role owns the coordination and data layer that makes that possible: keeping coaches briefed, ensuring they have current portfolio context, and flagging issues before they become problems.
Support Engagement Oversight. Reviews open support engagements and liaises with advisors and coaches to ensure hours approved in Impact OS align with logged hours. When invoices are submitted, verifies they tie to system records before moving to finance. Monitors AWS application approvals for portfolio companies.
Monthly Evaluation Processing. Works with Jaco through the monthly evaluation cycle — reviewing which companies are ready for 1:1 support, awaiting a seat, or not continuing — and manages the allocation of teams to coaches for the following month. Ensures companies awaiting a seat submit their monthly updates before evaluation week (last week of every month).
Builders & Community Strategy. Owns the health, growth, and experience of Volta's builder network across Atlantic Canada. Community strategy, experience architecture, province activation (NB, PEI, NL beyond NS), builder-leader relationships, health synthesis, and the Pulse data program. The community is not just an asset — it's a pipeline for the Lab and a co-founder pool for portfolio startups.
Sprint Program. Designs and oversees the Sprint cohort program — the structured 40-hour development pathway that turns community builders into vetted AI practitioners. Manages cohort design, lead builder coordination, and outcome tracking. Sprint is a new initiative with 37 people already waitlisted; this role brings it to life and scales it.
Builder-to-Co-Founder Pathway. Identifies high-potential builders in the network and creates structured pathways for the strongest Sprint graduates to be matched with portfolio startups as technical co-founders or early team members. Coordinates this pipeline with Jaco and portfolio coaches.
Community Health Reporting. Produces a monthly community health synthesis for Jaco and Matt — engagement data, Pulse scores, builder progress, event patterns, and strategic signals from the network. This report becomes a primary input for program design decisions, not a nice-to-have.
Lab Builder Pipeline. Serves as the bridge between the community and the SME AI Lab. Monthly sync with Andy Farnsworth to align builder readiness with Lab demand. The Lab's ability to hit its 26–32 engagement target depends on a healthy vetted builder supply — this role owns that supply side.

Who You Are
You are relational first — you build trust quickly, remember people, and understand that community health is made of individual relationships, not events.
You are data-comfortable — you can work in Impact OS, synthesise numbers into insights, and translate operational data into strategic signals for leadership.
You are a finisher — you follow through, close loops, and take satisfaction in systems that run cleanly and consistently.
You are comfortable operating with autonomy and ambiguity — Volta is a small team. You will often need to make judgment calls without a playbook and bring the playbook back to the team.
You are comfortable doing the work yourself — this is a small team. There is no execution layer between you and the output. The coaches deliver founder coaching; Amy delivers events. Everything else in this role's scope, you do directly.
Experience in community building, program management, or startup ecosystems is more relevant than any specific industry background. You don't need to have run a residency program before — but you need to be the kind of person who can learn a complex system quickly and run it reliably.

Role 02

Events & Community Operations Manager

Reports to: Jaco Burger, COO  ·  Full-time  ·  Halifax, NS

Purpose of this Role

Volta's community comes to life through well-executed events, consistent programming, and the operational infrastructure that makes engagement possible at scale. This role owns that execution layer — from flagship events to Sprint logistics to partner activations. Amy's superpower is delivery excellence. When scope is clear and she has what she needs, she executes brilliantly. This role is built around that strength — clear ownership, clear interfaces, and the room to contribute strategically within the events and programming domain.

Core Responsibilities
Event Strategy, Planning & Execution. Leads the strategy, planning, and delivery of all Volta-hosted events — founder programming, AI community meetups, partner activations, and external event bookings. Manages budgets, timelines, vendors, and logistics. Tracks performance and delivers post-event insights to leadership. This is the primary and highest-value focus of the role.
Sprint Program Logistics. Owns all operational delivery of the Sprint cohort program — scheduling, venue, tooling setup, session logistics, and builder-leader operational support. Works in close coordination with the Director of Community & Programs, who owns Sprint strategy and design.
Builder-Leader Operational Support. Provides hands-on operational support to builder-leaders running community events and meetups in Halifax and across provinces. Coordinates logistics, resources, and tooling so builder-leaders can focus on the community experience, not the admin.
Community Data Collection. Owns the collection of events and community data — registration numbers, attendance, engagement signals — and maintains accurate records in Impact OS and event platforms. This data feeds directly into the monthly community health report produced by the Director of Community & Programs.
Partner & Founder Event Support. Supports partnership conversations by coordinating event-level deliverables, activations, and logistics. Serves as the on-the-ground point of contact for partners during events and community initiatives. Works in collaboration with Matt on partnership execution, not as lead relationship owner.
Marketing & Communications — Events Focus. Creates and maintains event pages, promotional graphics, and social media content that highlights events, founder moments, partner involvement, and community activity. Maintains Volta's visual presence and brand consistency across event materials. Supports the broader communications strategy as it relates to events and programming.
Founder Initiative Execution. Supports and executes founder-facing initiatives in collaboration with the Director of Community & Programs — translating program ideas into concrete events, experiences, and touchpoints. Acts as a visible and approachable presence within the Volta community at events and in the space.

A Note on This Role

This role is not a narrowing — it's a sharpening. Amy's proposal correctly identified that events and programming are where her strongest contributions live. The arrival of a Director of Community & Programs doesn't diminish that; it removes the ambiguity that has made the role harder to do well. With clear scope, clear interfaces, and the operational space to focus on event excellence, this role has more impact potential, not less. The community events Volta runs — 10+ per month, 200–300+ registrations — are one of the organisation's most visible assets. That's this role's work.
