Structure & Role
Definitions

Three structural moves are converging: Jaco transitions to COO, Laura joins as Director of Community & Programs, and Amy's role is sharpened to Senior Manager, Community & Operations. This document defines each role from the ground up using a Jobs to Be Done lens — what each person exists to make happen — and maps the structure that results.

01

What's Changing

→
Jaco Burger: CINO → COO
Jaco has been operating at COO scope for months — running startup success, overseeing lab coordination, managing coaching, and carrying the operational load. The title change is a recognition, not a promotion. The trigger that makes it timing-right: Laura's hire removes the community and program coordination layer from Jaco's plate, creating the altitude he needs to truly operate as COO.
+
Laura Simpson: Director of Community & Programs
Originally interviewed for a narrower startup success role. Scope has expanded. Laura takes on three interconnected jobs: running the Residency program operations, owning the community and builder ecosystem strategy, and serving as the bridge between community talent and the Lab. She reports to Jaco. This is a hands-on role at a lean organisation — "Director" reflects scope, not separation from execution.
↑
Amy: Senior Manager, Community & Operations
Amy's title becomes "Senior Manager, Community & Operations" — acknowledging her experience and sharpening her scope to what she does brilliantly: events strategy through execution, Sprint logistics, and community operations. This is not a narrowing — it removes the ambiguity of carrying "acting community manager" alongside events manager. She gains a strategic partner in Laura, not a replacement. Reports to Laura.
$
Laurie: Finance & Compliance (Focused Scope)
Laurie's scope is explicitly finance and compliance only — financial statements, ACOA reporting, invoice processing, fiscal year close. The operations scope that previously sat ambiguously across Laurie and Jaco is now owned by Laura (program ops) and Amy (community ops). Laurie focuses exclusively on financial integrity, which is where the organisation needs her most, particularly with the April 30 three-year project close.
02

The Jobs Volta Needs Done

Why Jobs to Be Done? Org charts describe hierarchy. Job descriptions describe activities. Jobs to Be Done describes outcomes — what must actually happen for Volta's strategy to work. Each role below exists to get a specific job done. If the job isn't getting done, the role isn't working — regardless of how busy the person is.
1
Get founders to $1M ARR in 18 months
The primary reason Volta exists. Requires ruthless selection, relentless support for the 5%, real-time data visibility, and a coaching system that keeps improving. This job fails if selection is poor, if coaching is inconsistent, or if we don't have the data to catch problems before they become exits.
Job owned by: Jaco / COO with Laura running the program ops layer and Jared + coaching bench delivering coaching
2
Build and sustain Atlantic Canada's AI builder pipeline
251 builders aren't a community — they're a database. The job is to activate them into a functioning ecosystem: vetted practitioners, province-based leaders, Sprint graduates, and eventually co-founders. This job fails if the community is events-only with no development infrastructure or if builders engage but don't progress.
Job owned by: Laura for strategy and health, with Amy executing the event and operational layer
3
Prove AI ROI for Atlantic Canadian SMEs with documented impact
26–32 engagements, $2.5M+ documented economic impact, 66%+ success rate. This is Volta's differentiated methodology — problem-first, external partnerships, 90-day timelines, measurable outcomes. This job fails without a vetted builder supply from the community and without Andy having the bandwidth to manage engagements at scale.
Job owned by: Andy / Lab Director with Laura supplying the builder pipeline
4
Execute events and community programming with consistency and quality
10–15 events/month, Sprint cohort logistics, builder-leader operational support across provinces. This job fails if events feel disorganized, if Sprint logistics are chaotic, or if builder-leaders in NB/PEI/NL don't have the operational support to run their communities reliably.
Job owned by: Amy / Senior Manager executing within strategy set by Laura
5
Sustain funder relationships, organizational credibility, and financial health
ACOA reporting, Province of Nova Scotia relationship, board management, external positioning, and fiscal compliance. This job fails if Volta can't demonstrate measurable impact to funders, if financial reporting is late, or if the organisation's external credibility doesn't match what's happening internally.
Job owned by: Matt / CEO for relationships and strategy, with Laurie owning financial reporting and compliance

What Must Be True for This to Work

1
Laura's scope is lived, not just documented
The risk in any new hire is that Jaco keeps carrying the coordination layer out of habit. Laura needs real ownership from day one — which means Jaco actively transfers the briefing rhythms, data ops, and coach coordination within the first 30 days, not at some future point.
2
Amy and Laura's interface is clear from the start
The risk is ambiguity about who decides what — particularly on Sprint and community events. The scope document between Laura and Amy must be done within 100 days. Both need to understand that Amy executes within strategy Laura sets, and that this is a genuine partnership not a hierarchy of tasks.
3
Data is treated as infrastructure, not admin
Impact OS data consistency, Pulse response rates, and event data capture are not nice-to-haves. They are the operating system for go/no-go decisions, funder reporting, and coaching quality. This culture of data as infrastructure needs to be established in Q2 before it gets deprioritised for "real work."
4
The builder pipeline feeds the Lab reliably
Andy's 26–32 engagement target depends on a vetted builder supply. Laura and Andy need a monthly sync from day one so that builder development (Sprint, vetting, placement) is aligned with Lab demand. If this interface is ad-hoc, the Lab will be bottlenecked on talent and the community will produce activity without strategic output.
5
The COO title change happens cleanly
Jaco's title transition needs to land simultaneously with Laura's hire — not before, not months after. The narrative is: "Laura frees up Jaco to operate at COO altitude." If the title change happens first, there's a gap. If it happens after, the moment is lost.
6
Laurie's scope is protected
With an April 30 ACOA close and May 1 new fiscal year, Laurie is in a critical window. Any program operations scope that previously bled into her role needs to be explicitly handed to Laura — so Laurie can focus entirely on financial close without distractions.
