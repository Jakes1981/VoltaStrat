What This Role Exists To Do

Volta has three pillars and 300+ builders, and there hasn't been one person dedicated to making the community genuinely excellent. That changes with this role.

You own the human layer — the relationships, the data, the coaching infrastructure, and the experience of every person who engages with Volta's community.

Core Responsibilities
  1. 1 · Community Strategy & Experience
    You design and maintain the experience of Volta's builder community. That means the events, the communication rhythms, the builder-leader programme, and the overall health of how 300+ people relate to Volta.
  2. 2 · Builder-Leader Programme
    You manage relationships with Volta's builder-leaders — the community members who run local events and bring others in. These relationships are what makes the community self-sustaining.
  3. 3 · Coach Coordination
    You manage the contracted coaching bench for the Startup Residency. You own roster, briefings, scheduling, and 1:1 check-ins. Coaches report to you; Jaco leads their ongoing development and training.
  4. 4 · Community Data Quality
    You are responsible for the accuracy and currency of Volta's community data in Impact OS and Pulse. Jaco and Matt read health data directly from the system — your job is to make sure what they see is real, current, and complete.
  5. 5 · Sprint Community Synthesis
    You track builder demand and experience within Sprint, surface patterns and insights to Matt, and ensure the community lens is represented in Sprint planning. You do not own Sprint design — Matt does.
The Day-to-Day
70–75% of your time

Relationships, strategy, and synthesis — the human and intellectual work that is the core of this role.

25–30% of your time

Operational tasks — application processing, onboarding, coach invoicing. Volta's AI stack handles the heavy lifting, but these tasks are yours to own.

What We're Looking For
  • A community builder — you've built genuine relationships at scale and know what makes people feel part of something
  • A structured thinker — you can hold complexity and create order without losing the human element
  • A data owner — you understand that good decisions come from clean data, and you take ownership of the systems
  • An operator — you follow through. You don't drop balls.
