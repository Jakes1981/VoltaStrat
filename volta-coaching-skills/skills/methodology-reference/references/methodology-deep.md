# Volta Coaching Methodology Reference

Condensed from analysis of 356 coaching sessions. Used by the `/coach-prep` skill to generate stage-appropriate coaching guidance.

## The Three Pillars

### 1. Problem Hypothesis

Template: "We believe that [WHO] experiences [NEGATIVE IMPACT] when [TRIGGER] happens because of [ROOT CAUSE]. Alleviating that pain would allow them to achieve [FUTURE STATE]."

Components:
- **Who:** Specific person/role, not a category ("gym owners with 150-300 members" not "fitness businesses")
- **Trigger:** The moment they realize they have the problem
- **Problem:** From the customer's perspective — they could read it and say "yes, exactly"
- **Impact:** Quantifiable where possible (lost revenue, wasted time, regulatory risk)
- **Root Cause:** Deeper than the symptom
- **Future State:** Customer's world after, not what the product does

Problem statement work is ITERATIVE. Expect 2-3 rounds of refinement based on customer interview data. Use problem affinity scoring (count signal matches per interview) and satisfaction vs urgency gap analysis.

### 2. Risk Analysis (Continuous)

Four risks scored 1-10, tracked throughout the journey:

| Risk | Question | Evidence Sources |
|------|----------|-----------------|
| Value | Will customers pay for this? | Customer count, revenue, interviews, LOIs, churn |
| Feasibility | Can this team build it? | Shipping cadence, technical depth, milestone progression |
| Usability | Will users actually adopt it? | Retention, engagement, onboarding completion, NPS |
| Viability | Can this become a business? | MRR trajectory, burn rate, runway, pricing, market size |

Risk focus shifts by stage:
- Value Baseline: 90% Value Risk
- First Customer: Value → Viability
- $10K ARR: Feasibility + Viability
- $100K ARR: Viability + Usability
- $1M ARR: All low, institutional risks emerge

### 3. Milestone Progression

| Stage | Revenue Marker | Evidence Required |
|-------|---------------|-------------------|
| Value Baseline | Pre-revenue | 5+ customer discovery interviews, problem validated |
| First Paying Customer | First $ | Demo conversion, someone paid, repeatable path emerging |
| X ($10K ARR) | $10K recurring | Outbound activity, pipeline metrics, growth engine forming |
| 10X ($100K ARR) | $100K @ 20% MoM | Sustained growth, team beyond founders, market position clear |
| $1M ARR | $1M @ 20% MoM | Graduation-ready, institutional readiness |

## Stage-Specific Coaching Guide

### Value Baseline
**Objective:** Validate the problem exists and someone will pay
**Coach Focus:** Problem statement refinement, customer interview design, anti-bias methodology
**Key Questions:** "Tell me the problem from the customer's perspective — not the solution." / "How many people outside your network have you spoken to?" / "What surprised you?"
**Experiments:** 10-20 structured interviews, problem affinity scoring, demo commitment question
**Traps:** Solution-first thinking, network-only interviews, building before validating
**Cadence:** Weekly or bi-weekly, hands-on

### First Paying Customer
**Objective:** Prove someone will exchange money for this
**Coach Focus:** Pricing, conversion mechanics, early unit economics
**Key Questions:** "What did they actually pay for?" / "How did they find you?" / "What's the lifetime value vs churn cost?"
**Experiments:** Pricing tests, beta with 3-5 specific customers, channel experiments
**Traps:** Underpricing, building features instead of selling, one-off revenue
**Cadence:** Bi-weekly

### X ($10K ARR)
**Objective:** Build a repeatable growth engine
**Coach Focus:** Growth mechanics, channel optimization, team structure
**Key Questions:** "What's your growth rate?" / "What's your primary channel?" / "Who does sales full-time?"
**Experiments:** Multi-channel with per-channel CAC, landing page tests, referral programs
**Traps:** Too many channels, hiring too early, ignoring churn
**Cadence:** Bi-weekly, shifting strategic

### 10X ($100K ARR)
**Objective:** Prove this is scalable, not lifestyle
**Coach Focus:** Market positioning, hiring, investment readiness. Consider Foundation Sprint if strategic reset needed.
**Key Questions:** "Lifestyle or venture?" / "What's your hiring plan?" / "Are you investment-ready?"
**Experiments:** Positioning reframes, first non-founder hire, conference pitch tests
**Traps:** Founder burnout, not delegating, identity crisis
**Cadence:** Ad-hoc, strategic sessions only

### $1M ARR
**Objective:** Graduation preparation
**Coach Focus:** Institutional readiness, board, organizational design
**Cadence:** Ad-hoc, connector role

## Experiment Design Standard

Every experiment must have:
1. **Hypothesis:** "We believe [action] will result in [outcome] for [audience]"
2. **Metric:** What specific number will tell us if it worked?
3. **Timeline:** By when? (2 weeks typical for early stage, 30 days for growth)
4. **Decision Threshold:** "If we see X, we continue. If not, we pivot."

## Accountability Model

- **Bidirectional commitments:** Coach AND founder have action items every session
- **Minimum commitment:** 20% MoM growth as evidence floor; 5 discovery transcripts / 5 demos per 2 weeks for early stage
- **Off-ramp language:** Companies that don't meet minimums aren't "fired" — they're moved to off-ramp (community access, events, re-engagement possible)
- **Escalation path:** Support → tighter metrics → viability discussion → compassionate off-ramp

## Red Flags to Surface in Prep Briefs

Flag these in pre-session notes when detected in company data:
- No company update in 30+ days
- No coaching session in 21+ days
- Problem statement changes every session without convergence
- Revenue pipeline discussed but never converts
- All team members are unpaid
- Founder splitting time across multiple ventures
- Building features instead of talking to customers
- High satisfaction with status quo among interviewees

## Coaching Arc

When generating prep briefs, note where the company is in the coaching arc:
- **Intensive:** Weekly, hands-on (Value Baseline stage)
- **Active:** Bi-weekly, balanced (First Customer → X stage)
- **Loosening:** Bi-weekly to ad-hoc (X → 10X stage)
- **Ad-hoc:** Slack-based, strategic only ($100K+ stage)

If a company's session frequency doesn't match their stage, flag it as a data point.

## Foundation Sprint (Optional Tool)

Consider deploying when:
- New resident arrives without clear problem hypothesis or 90-day plan (not required for all)
- Company at $100K+ needs strategic reset
- Company stuck — standard coaching isn't producing movement
- Before major decision (expansion, fundraising, pivot)

Note: Not a required part of every engagement. Some teams arrive ready and don't need it. See "Click" by Jake Knapp & John Zeratsky for the methodology.

Structure: Customer segmentation → Problem mapping → Competitor analysis → Capabilities → Differentiation mapping → Values → Solution options → 90-day plan
