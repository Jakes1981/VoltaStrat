Monthly Evaluation Cycle

This is the heartbeat of Volta's 1:1 residency program. Every month, the team reviews current residents and eligible candidates, makes seat allocation decisions, and closes the loop with founders. This entire cycle must be completed by the first day of the next month — no gaps, no breaks in coaching coverage.

Step-by-Step Process

01 Email Eligible Teams
  • Send progress update request to all companies on the eligibility list
  • Request: current progress, metrics, continued interest in a 1:1 seat
  • Source: eligibility list in Impact OS (Supabase)
  • Sent from: residency@voltaeffect.com
  • Deadline: end of week 3
02 Collect & Curate Updates
  • Gather all responses from eligible teams
  • Curate into the monthly review document
  • Flag which teams should be considered based on available seats
Note: Not all eligible teams will be considered — depends on seat availability.
03 Review Expiring Seats
  • Identify all current 1:1 residents whose 3-month engagement ends this month
  • Each coach reviews their own teams and prepares a case for renewal
  • Coach must be prepared to answer questions from Matt and Laura
04 Risk Assessment — Current Residents
  • Grade each team on four dimensions, each scored out of 10
Value Risk
Is the problem worth solving? Is there genuine demand?
Feasibility Risk
Can this team actually build it? Technical capability, resources, timeline?
Usability Risk
Will users actually use it? UX, adoption friction, behavior change required?
Viability Risk
Can this become a sustainable business? Unit economics, market size, defensibility?
Note: Teams with users and paying customers should objectively score better than those without. The risk framework rewards evidence over aspiration.
05 Risk Assessment — New Eligible Teams
  • Apply the same 4-risk framework to new candidates
  • Acknowledge: most new seats will score high-risk across all dimensions (early stage)
  • Differentiate based on: quality of progress update, evidence of traction, founder clarity
06 Decision Meeting — Laura + Matt
  • Laura and Matt review all assessments together
  • Coaches present their cases and answer questions
  • Decisions made: renew, exit, or allocate to new team
  • Laura coordinates, Matt provides strategic oversight
Note: Jaco may be available for the first few months as contracted support, outside Platform Calgary hours.
07 Close the Loop — Rejected Teams
  • Mark rejected teams in the eligibility list (Impact OS)
  • Send personalized rejection email from the system
  • Sent from: residency@voltaeffect.com
Important: Each rejection email should include a personalized note with genuine feedback and advice. This is a small lift that adds real value — these founders took time to update us, and a thoughtful response maintains the relationship.
08 Close the Loop — Accepted Teams
  • Create new engagement in Impact OS for new teams from eligibility list and renewed teams from existing residents
  • Send acceptance email from the system
  • Assign coach
  • Introduction email goes to both coach and startup founder on record
  • Purpose: schedule the first (or next) coaching call
Critical: This is critical to ensure work continues without breaks and gaps.
09 Verify Completion
  • All rejected teams notified with personalized feedback
  • All accepted teams have active engagements in Impact OS
  • All coaches know their assignments
  • Introduction emails sent and first calls being scheduled
  • Complete by day 1 of the next month
03

The Risk Framework

Four-Dimension Risk Assessment
Each dimension scored 1–10. Lower score = lower risk = stronger position.
Score Range Meaning
1 – 3 Low risk — strong evidence, clear path
4 – 6 Moderate risk — some evidence, questions remain
7 – 10 High risk — limited evidence, significant uncertainty
The framework is a guide, not a formula. Coaches bring context that numbers can't capture. The score informs the conversation — it doesn't make the decision. Laura and Matt make the final call.
