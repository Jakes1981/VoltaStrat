02

Stage 1 Screening — The Gate

Stage 1 is a hard gate. It filters applications against minimum eligibility criteria before any interview time is invested. Each criterion is scored +1 / 0 / −1. A single −1 on Location, AI-First, or Disallowed Category results in an automatic No-Go.

Auto No-Go if −1
Location: Atlantic Canada
  • +1 At least one full-time founder based in Atlantic Canada; PR/work-permit verified if not a citizen
  • 0 Some presence or intent but not full-time; immigration status unclear
  • −1 No founder based in region; incompatible immigration status
Auto No-Go if −1
AI-First, Software-Only
  • +1 AI is core to value or materially accelerates build/iteration; stack described; software-only
  • 0 Mentions AI but peripheral or unproven; unclear stack
  • −1 Agency/service; hardware or hardware+software; regulated industry
Flags for Review
Problem ↔ Solution Fit
  • +1 Specific user/ICP and pain articulated; solution maps directly; early signal (users, LOIs, discovery notes)
  • 0 Problem stated but ICP/pain light; weak mapping to solution
  • −1 Vague problem; solution-first buzzwords; compliance-only drivers
Auto No-Go if −1
Disallowed Category Check
  • +1 Clearly outside disallowed categories
  • 0 Mixed signals or pending pivot
  • −1 Agency/service, hardware, or regulated (fintech, medtech, insurtech, legaltech, cannabis, gambling, aviation, etc.)
Gate Rule: A single −1 on Location, AI-First Software, or Disallowed Category = immediate No-Go. No interview. Send rejection email with specific feedback and referral to relevant resources. Problem-Solution Fit at −1 does not auto-reject but flags for further review before proceeding.

Stage 1 deliverables:

  • Narrative assessment (internal) with rationale and risks
  • Two-paragraph summary for Impact OS
  • Go / No-Go decision

If Go → schedule Founder & Mindset Fit interview. If No-Go → send rejection email with specific feedback + referral.

Pathway Decision — Three Branches

Network (1:Many)
Community + Support
The default positive path. All AI-first founders who pass Stage 1 and complete a mindset chat enter the Network. This is not a structured program — it is community access and connection.
  • Workspace at Volta
  • Founder Connect Days (1–2×/month)
  • Monthly CEO Breakfasts, Run Club, Hockey
  • Partner Office Hours
  • AI Mixers, showcases, peer meetups
  • Perks toolkit and credits
Owner: Lindsay
Residency (1:1)
Coaching + Accountability
Selective pathway for founders with traction evidence and strong interview scores. Starts with a Foundation Sprint and leads to dedicated 1:1 coaching.
  • Foundation Sprint onboarding
  • 1:1 performance coaching (5–10 hrs/month)
  • Milestone accountability & weekly rhythm
  • Selective resource support (experiments, SME projects)
  • Recommended reading: “Click” by Knapp & Zeratsky
Owner: Startup Success
No-Go
Rejection with Feedback
Fails Stage 1 gate criteria or interview threshold not met. Every No-Go receives specific, honest feedback and relevant referrals.
  • Specific rejection email explaining the reason
  • Referral to relevant resources and programs
  • CC Lindsay for ecosystem introductions
  • Encouragement to reapply if circumstances change
Owner: Startup Success + Lindsay
Interview Guide: For the detailed conversational framework, scoring rubric (1–5 across 5 dimensions), and interactive scorecard used during the interview itself, see the Founder & Mindset Fit Interview Guide (V5.0). This page covers when and why to interview — that page covers how to conduct it.

Stage 1 Screening Rubric

Two scoring systems exist. The +1/0/−1 rubric on this page is for initial application screening — fast, binary decisions on minimum criteria. The 1–5 scoring in the Interview Guide is for the in-depth interview conversation. Screening comes first; the interview happens later for candidates who pass.
Criterion 1
Location: Atlantic Canada
+1At least one full-time founder headquartered or primarily operating in Atlantic Canada. PR/work-permit verified if not a Canadian citizen.
0Founder is Atlantic Canada-based but company still forming, or status documentation is unclear and needs follow-up.
−1No founder based in the region. Incompatible immigration status. Auto No-Go
Criterion 2
AI-First, Software-Only
+1AI is core to the product's value proposition or materially accelerates build/iteration. Technical stack described. Software-only.
0AI is mentioned but peripheral or unproven. Unclear technical stack. Potential but needs validation.
−1Agency or service company. Hardware or hardware+software. AI is cosmetic or marketing-only. Auto No-Go
Criterion 3
Problem ↔ Solution Fit
+1Specific user/ICP and pain clearly articulated. Solution maps directly to the problem. Early signal present (users, LOIs, discovery notes).
0Problem stated but ICP or pain is light. Weak mapping between problem and proposed solution.
−1Vague problem. Solution-first buzzwords. Compliance-only drivers. Flags for review but does not auto-reject.
Criterion 4
Disallowed Category Check
+1Clearly outside all disallowed categories. Standard software/SaaS.
0Mixed signals or pending pivot. Edge case that needs deeper review.
−1Agency/service, hardware, or regulated industry: fintech, medtech, insurtech, legaltech, cannabis, gambling, aviation. Auto No-Go

Network to Residency — Upgrade Path

Founders placed in the Network are reviewed monthly for upgrade to Residency. This is not a waiting list — founders must demonstrate concrete progress. The same +1/0/−1 anchors are used.

#Criterion+10−1
1Consistent effort toward $1M+ ARRDisciplined cadence tied to $1M pathEffort present but needs prodding; inconsistentSporadic; shifting priorities
2Effort → Results (revenue growth)Clear revenue growth trajectoryOutcomes below expectations for model/marketMotion without outcomes
3Dissatisfied with current paceSelf-directed urgency to accelerateOnly when challengedComplacent
4Resources available (e.g., capital)Flexible runway/resources to scale nowResources tied to narrow projectsNone; no plan to unlock
5Fast product iterationShip–learn–ship rhythm; fast cyclesDelays/disconnect with prioritiesLong cycles; backlog stasis
6Founders full-timeAll core founders full-timeSome founders FT; others splitSide-projecting
7All founders engaged in growthVisible contributions from allUneven effort across foundersMissing/weak contributions
8Proactive engagement with coachProactive; 2+ touchpoints/weekResponsive but slower cadenceReactive or absent
9Revenue objective in playWorking explicitly toward $1M ARR+“10X” stretch but sub-$1M path“X or lower”
Upgrade Rule: Interview total ≥ +3 AND Readiness total ≥ +3 (last 30–60 days), plus Residency capacity available ⇒ schedule Foundation Sprint. Laura coordinates the monthly upgrade review during Week 3, aligned with the Monthly Evaluation Cycle.

Communication & System Integration

All application communications are managed through the Coach Portal with templated emails sent directly from the system. Email send history is connected to company records in Supabase — Laura can query sent communications per company.

Email sequence stages:

  1. Application Confirmation — Auto-acknowledgment on receipt
  2. Interview Invite — Sent when founder passes Stage 1 screening
  3. Network Acceptance — Welcome to 1:Many community with Lindsay onboarding
  4. Residency Acceptance — Welcome + Foundation Sprint booking link
  5. Waitlist — Strong signals but at capacity; monthly upgrade review
  6. Rejection: Incomplete — Insufficient context; invite to reapply
  7. Rejection: Not AI-First — Outside focus area; alternative referrals
  8. Rejection: Hardware/Regulated — Specialized org referrals
  9. Rejection: Outside Atlantic Canada — Eligibility note; invite if circumstances change
  10. Monthly Upgrade Evidence Request — Fresh evidence for Network → Residency review
  11. No-Show Nudge — Rescheduling prompt
  12. Lindsay Onboarding Follow-up — Resource pack, community calendar, intro facilitation
Template management: Email templates are stored in the Coach Portal with one-click send and auto-filled personalization fields. For template updates or new templates, edit them directly in the Coach Portal codebase. Email data is connected to company records via Supabase for audit and querying.

Coach Portal & System Reference

The application workflow is implemented in the Coach Portal (impact-os-coach). Key system integration points:

  • Kanban Board — 5 columns mapping to application stages: Review, Invited to Interview, Interviewed, Accepted, Rejected
  • Email Sending — Templates previewed and sent from the portal with personalization fields auto-filled from application data
  • Supabase Table — applications table with data JSONB field for rubric scores and status tracked via timestamp fields (submitted_at, invited_at, interviewed_at, approved_at, declined_at)
  • Interview Scorecard — The Interview Guide page’s scorecard saves directly to the application record via the portal API
