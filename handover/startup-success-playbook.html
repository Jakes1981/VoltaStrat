Section 01
The Residency Model

Volta's 1:1 residency provides dedicated coaching to founders through 3-month engagement cycles. Seats are limited and allocated based on evidence of traction and potential.

Each resident is paired with a coach who meets with them regularly. The goal: help founders progress through Volta's milestone framework toward $1M ARR.

The milestone framework defines where a company sits and what they need to achieve next:

1
Value Baseline
Pre-revenue. 5+ customer discovery interviews completed. Problem-solution fit emerging.
2
First Paying Customers
Demo conversion happening. First revenue collected. Evidence of willingness to pay.
3
X Stage (5-10k ARR)
Outbound activity in motion. Pipeline metrics being tracked. Repeatable sales motion forming.
4
100k ARR
20%+ month-over-month growth. Product-market fit signals strong. Revenue engine running.
5
1M ARR
Sustained growth at scale. Graduation from the residency program. Mission accomplished.
Section 02
Application & Eligibility Pipeline

Applications come through Impact OS. The pipeline tracks every team from first submission through to a seat decision or decline.

Application stages flow as follows: submitted → invited → interviewed → approved → declined. Approved applications land on the eligibility list — the pool from which new seats are filled each month.

Laura manages the pipeline, ensures data quality, and coordinates outreach to eligible teams during each monthly evaluation cycle.

Section 04
Monthly Operations Calendar

A month-at-a-glance showing what happens when. Every month follows this rhythm:

Week 1
  • New month begins — all engagements should be active
  • Coaches begin sessions with assigned companies
  • Verify no gaps in coverage from previous month's decisions
Week 2
  • Company updates being collected (monthly submission requirement)
  • Monitor submission rates — follow up with companies that haven't submitted
  • Coach check-ins happening
Week 3
  • Eligible team outreach sent (progress updates + interest)
  • Company update deadline
  • Eligible team response deadline (end of week)
Week 4
  • Monthly evaluation cycle (see dedicated page)
  • Risk assessments completed
  • Seat decisions made
  • Acceptance/rejection communications sent
  • New engagements created in Impact OS
  • All complete by day 1 of next month
Section 05
Engagement Lifecycle

A single engagement from creation to close follows these steps:

1
New Engagement Created
Team accepted from eligibility list or renewed for another cycle.
2
Coach Assigned
Based on availability, expertise, and team needs.
3
Introduction Email
Sent to coach + founder. Purpose is to schedule the first call.
4
Coaching Sessions Begin
Regular cadence established — typically bi-weekly or weekly.
5
Sessions Recorded
notetaker@voltaeffect.com added to meetings for Fireflies transcription.
6
Company Updates
Monthly submissions with metrics: MRR, customers, cash, burn, runway.
7
Monthly Review
Coach assesses progress, presents at evaluation meeting.
8
3-Month Decision Point
Renew for another 3 months, or exit the program.
9
Exit
Team graduates, stalls, or seat is better allocated elsewhere.
Section 06
Support & Financial Administration
  • AWS Credits — application and tracking process for portfolio companies. Monitor expiration dates and usage.
  • Invoice Reconciliation — coach hours logged vs. approved vs. invoiced. Every invoice should match what was approved in Impact OS.
  • Hours Tracking — verification that coach hours match approved allocations in Impact OS. Flag discrepancies before payment.
  • Financial Oversight — Lori (VP Finance, part-time) handles ACOA reporting and financial statements. Coordinate with her on budget questions.
