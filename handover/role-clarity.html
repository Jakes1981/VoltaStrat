The Galvanizing Gap

Jay’s departure earlier in 2026 created a “galvanizing” gap — the urgency, enthusiasm, and momentum-driving energy that keeps execution moving. Jaco’s departure compounds this. Matt and Laura are competent leaders, but neither is a natural galvanizer in the Working Genius sense.
This is not a criticism — it’s a strategic awareness. The team should:
  • Build galvanizing energy into team rituals (celebrate wins publicly, maintain urgency in standups)
  • Use the monthly evaluation cycle as a forcing function for momentum
  • Lean on the builder community and events (Amy’s domain) for external energy
  • Monitor for execution drift — if things slow down, name it early

Clarity prevents drift. When everyone knows what they own and who decides, the team moves faster with less friction. Refer back to this page whenever there’s ambiguity about ownership or authority. Update it as the team evolves.
