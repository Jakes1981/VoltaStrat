AI Landscape for Coaches

What matters in AI-first startups right now. Use this to assess whether a founder's AI approach is genuinely differentiated or just riding the wave.

Last updated: Q2 2026
01 What Matters Now
1
Build Speed Has Collapsed

An MVP can ship in a weekend using AI coding tools (Cursor, Claude Code, Bolt). This means: building is no longer the differentiator. Distribution and sales ARE the differentiator. When assessing founders, weight their ability to sell and find customers higher than their ability to build.

Assess distribution and sales capability above technical ability.
2
AI Capabilities Are Commodity

Every no-code tool has AI. Every SaaS adds "AI-powered" features. The question is no longer "does this use AI?" It's: "Given that everyone has AI, what is this founder's non-AI moat?" Look for: proprietary data, unique workflow integration, domain expertise, regulatory positioning, distribution advantage.

The moat is never the AI itself — it's what surrounds it.
3
From Prediction to Agents

The shift from "AI as classification/prediction" to "AI as autonomous agent" is accelerating. Products that simply present AI outputs (dashboards, recommendations) are already dated. Look for founders building AI that acts — agents that complete tasks, make decisions, and learn from outcomes.

AI that acts > AI that suggests.
02 AI Assessment Questions
"What happens to your product if you remove the AI layer? Does it still work?"
"If someone with access to the same foundation models started building against you tomorrow, why do you still win?"
"What's changed in AI in the last 6 months that affects what you're building?"
"If you were starting from scratch today, would you build this the same way?"
"What tools are you building with?"
Cursor, Claude Code, v0 = good signals. Not using any AI tools = concern.
"Is your moat in the model, the data, or the workflow?"
"How much of your value comes from AI vs domain expertise?"
03 Common Patterns
What Works
  • AI deeply integrated into a specific workflow (not bolted on)
  • Proprietary data that improves with usage
  • Domain expertise + AI (not AI alone)
  • Founder who builds with AI tools themselves
  • Products where removing AI breaks everything
What Doesn't Work
  • "AI wrapper" products — a UI on top of a foundation model API with no proprietary value
  • AI as a marketing claim, not a technical reality
  • Products that could exist without AI (rules-based logic would work)
  • Founder who doesn't use AI tools in their own development process
  • Competitive moat that depends entirely on having access to a specific model
