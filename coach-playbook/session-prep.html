Session Prep

Your Session Workflow

Three phases for every coaching session. Before: know where they are. During: focus on what matters. After: close the loop. This workflow ensures every session moves the founder forward.

01 Before the Session Pre-Session
✓
Know the founder's current milestone stage
Check their last update in Impact OS. Are they Value Baseline, First Customer, $10K, $100K, or $1M? This determines your entire coaching focus. → Stage Guide
✓
Review commitments from last session
What did the founder commit to? What did you commit to? Open action items should be the first thing you discuss.
✓
Check latest metrics / company update
MRR, customers, burn rate, runway. Has anything changed materially since last session? Flag surprises.
✓
Identify the primary risk to probe
At their stage, which of the 4 risks (Value, Feasibility, Usability, Viability) should you focus on? → Risk Framework
✓
Prepare 2–3 key questions
Don't wing it. Based on the stage and their recent progress, what specific questions will produce the most signal?
✓
Verify Fireflies is recording
notetaker@voltaeffect.com should be on the calendar invite. If not, add it or remind the founder.

This checklist resets each time you reload the page. It's a session tool, not a tracker.

02 During the Session In-Session
Opening · 5 min
"What's happened since last time?"

Let the founder lead. Listen for what they're proud of and what they're avoiding.

Then: review commitments from last session. Did they do what they said they'd do? Did you? If commitments were missed, don't lecture — probe why. The WHY is more diagnostic than the miss.

Middle · 20–25 min

Focus on the primary risk for their stage. Don't try to cover everything — pick the ONE thing that matters most today.

Listen more than you talk. The founder should be speaking 70%+ of the time.
Coach in real time. When you see an opportunity to reframe, challenge, or suggest — take it. The founder's response tells you everything about coachability.
One positive response isn't enough. If it took four probes from different angles to get them to engage with your challenge, that's a signal.
"Don't tell me the solution" is your sharpest tool. When a founder can't describe the problem without describing their product, they need more customer discovery.
Common Session Scenarios
The founder is stuck ▼
Go back to the problem statement. Ask: "Can you explain the problem from your customer's perspective?" If they can't, the problem statement needs work. The stuck feeling usually means they've lost sight of the customer problem and are solving for their own assumptions. → Problem Hypothesis guide
The founder has no metrics ▼
Prescribe an experiment. Every experiment needs: hypothesis, metric, timeline, decision threshold. No metrics = no evidence = no progress. If they don't know what to measure, they don't know what they're testing. → Experiments catalog
The founder is building, not selling ▼
Challenge directly: "Who on the team is spending the majority of time on sales?" If the answer is "nobody" or "I will once the product is ready," this is a red flag. Redirect to customer conversations immediately. Building without selling is the most common way early-stage founders avoid market reality.
The founder is emotional / burnout signs ▼
Founder wellness is a business variable. Address it directly. "How are you doing — not the company, you?" Share your own experiences if appropriate. Recommend "Buying Back Your Time" (Dan Martell) if they're drowning in tasks. This is not a distraction from coaching — it IS coaching.
The founder wants to show me their product ▼
Redirect: "Before we look at the product, tell me about the problem." Product demos eat session time without producing coaching signal. The problem and the market are what matter. If the product is relevant, you'll get there — but the problem comes first.
Closing · 5 min

Set commitments — BOTH ways.

"What are you going to do before our next session?" — Founder commits
"What I'll do for you: [introduction, resource, follow-up]" — Coach commits
"When's our next session?" — Confirm cadence
Minimum standard: No session ends without at least one clear commitment from the founder.
03 After the Session Post-Session
✓
Fireflies transcript will sync automatically
Check that the recording processed in Supabase (meeting_transcripts table). If not, the notetaker may not have been added.
✓
Send recap to the founder
Brief email: what was discussed, founder's commitments, your commitments, next session date. Keep it under 200 words. Direct, personal, no corporate language.
✓
Update company observations in Impact OS
Add any notable observations, risk changes, or concerns.
✓
Flag for review if needed
If the founder is stalling, showing burnout signs, or the company needs a seat evaluation discussion, flag it for Laura/Matt.
04 Fireflies Setup Technical
Volta's Fireflies account is connected to notetaker@voltaeffect.com
Add this email as an attendee on the Google Calendar invite for every coaching session
The AI notetaker joins automatically and records the meeting
Transcripts sync to Supabase and are available in the coach portal
⚠
If a founder asks the notetaker to leave ("remove the note taker", "kick out the bot"), everything after that point is off-record. Do not reference off-record content in any follow-up, report, or documentation. When in doubt, less is more.

Every session is an opportunity to move a founder forward. The prep ensures you're focused. The structure ensures nothing gets missed. The follow-up ensures progress compounds. Run this workflow consistently and the coaching writes itself.
