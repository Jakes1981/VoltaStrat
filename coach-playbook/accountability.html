Accountability

Accountability Framework

Coaching without accountability is just conversation. Every session produces commitments. Every commitment gets tracked. When founders struggle, there's a clear path.

01 Bidirectional Commitments

Every session produces commitments from BOTH sides.

Coach Commitments

Introductions, resources, analysis, follow-up actions.

"I'll connect you with [person] this week."
"I'll review your pricing model and send feedback."
Founder Commitments

Experiments to run, conversations to have, data to collect, milestones to hit.

"I'll conduct 10 customer interviews by our next session."
"I'll test the new pricing with 5 prospects."

When you commit alongside the founder, you create mutual accountability. You're not assigning homework — you're partnering on progress.

Minimum standard: No session should end without at least one clear commitment from the founder.
02 The 20% MoM Standard

The minimum evidence floor for continued coaching investment.

What It Means

Once a company has paying customers, they should demonstrate 20% month-over-month growth on at least one key metric — revenue, users, or a leading indicator they're tracking.

When It Applies

Post-first-paying-customer. Not applicable at Value Baseline, where the metric is "customer conversations completed."

What It's NOT

A punishment threshold. It's an evidence standard — proof that coaching investment is translating into progress.

What to Do When Founders Miss It

Don't lecture. Probe: "What happened? What blocked progress? Do we need to change the approach?" If the miss is a one-time event, course-correct. If it's a pattern across 2–3 months, escalate.

For early stage (Value Baseline): The equivalent standard is 5 sales discovery transcripts every 2 weeks + 5 qualified demos every 2 weeks.

03 The Escalation Path
1
Support

Default mode. Regular coaching cadence. Founder is progressing.

2
Tighter Metrics

Founder has missed commitments or growth is stalling. Impose shorter timelines and more specific deliverables. Example: "5 transcripts in 2 weeks, 15 in 4 weeks, 5 demos in 4 weeks." Check in more frequently.

3
Viability Discussion

Pattern of missed commitments over 2+ months. Have a direct conversation about whether the current approach is working. "The evidence says X. What do you think we should do differently?" This is NOT a threat — it's an honest assessment.

4
Compassionate Off-Ramp

After sustained lack of progress despite tighter metrics and direct conversation. The founder is moved to community access (events, programming) but coaching resources are redirected. This is NOT an exit — it preserves the relationship.

Important: There is no "you should shut down" conversation in Volta coaching. The program structure (3-month engagement windows, off-ramps, community access) creates natural transition points that preserve relationship capital.
04 The Off-Ramp
What It IS
  • A structured transition from intensive coaching to community membership
  • Founder retains access to events, programming, and the network
  • Can re-engage if circumstances change materially
What It IS NOT
  • A rejection
  • A judgment on the founder
  • A permanent door-closing
  • A conversation to dread
Language That Works
"This isn't about failure — it's about fit and timing."
"The evidence suggests this isn't the right moment for intensive coaching. Let's keep you connected to the community."
"If things change — new traction, new team member, pivot that's working — reach out. We'll reassess."
Language to Avoid
"You're not performing."
Too judgmental
"We're cutting you."
Too aggressive
"Maybe entrepreneurship isn't for you."
Never say this
05 3-Month Engagement Windows

How the structured cycle creates natural accountability.

Every coaching engagement is a 3-month commitment. This creates a shared understanding of the investment horizon.
At the end of each 3-month window: renewal decision based on risk assessment and coach recommendation.
Natural checkpoints. Founders know there's an evaluation, coaches know there's a decision point.
Renewal is not automatic — it requires evidence of progress.

Accountability is the infrastructure that turns coaching into results. Without it, sessions become pleasant conversations that don't move anything forward. Run the commitments, track the evidence, and have the hard conversations when the data demands them.
