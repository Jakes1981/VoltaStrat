Risk Framework

Four Risks, One Framework

Four dimensions of risk run continuously throughout a founder's journey. They shift in priority by stage but never disappear. Use this framework to diagnose where to focus coaching energy.

01 The Four Risks
Value Risk
Is the problem worth solving? Is there genuine demand?
Low (1–3): Paying customers, growing usage, clear pain evidence
Medium (4–6): Some evidence, questions remain
High (7–10): Limited evidence, unclear if problem is real
Evidence Sources
Customer count, revenue, interviews, LOIs, churn
Feasibility Risk
Can this team actually build it?
Low (1–3): Shipping consistently, technical depth proven
Medium (4–6): Capability exists but execution inconsistent
High (7–10): Significant capability gaps, can't ship
Evidence Sources
Shipping cadence, product iteration speed, team composition
Usability Risk
Will users actually use it?
Low (1–3): Active engaged users, retention strong, friction low
Medium (4–6): Some adoption but friction exists, drop-off
High (7–10): Significant behavior change required, complex onboarding
Evidence Sources
Active users, retention/churn, onboarding completion, NPS
Viability Risk
Can this become a sustainable business?
Low (1–3): Unit economics work, clear path to sustainability
Medium (4–6): Path exists but unproven
High (7–10): Business model undefined, tiny market, no pricing power
Evidence Sources
MRR trajectory, burn rate, runway, pricing model, market size
02 How Risk Shifts by Stage
Stage Primary Risk Why
Value Baseline 90% Value Risk “Does anyone actually have this problem?”
First Customer Value → Viability “People will pay, but can the economics work?”
$10K ARR Feasibility + Viability “Can we scale the team and operations?”
$100K ARR Viability + Usability “Is this a venture-scale business?”
$1M ARR All risks low New institutional risks emerge
03 How to Assess in Practice
When you ask… You're assessing…
“Have customers shown willingness to pay?” Value Risk
“How many discovery conversations have you had?” Value Risk
“Can the team ship this in the next 2 weeks?” Feasibility Risk
“What's your shipping cadence?” Feasibility Risk
“Are users actually logging in and using it?” Usability Risk
“What's your onboarding completion rate?” Usability Risk
“Do the unit economics work?” Viability Risk
“What's your burn rate vs runway?” Viability Risk
“What's your CAC:LTV ratio?” Viability Risk

Risk analysis is embedded in every coaching session but rarely named explicitly. Only 1 in 29 primary success company sessions used the term “value risk” by name. Coaches assess risk through questions, not through the framework vocabulary. The vocabulary is for evaluation; the questions are for coaching.

This tool doesn't save data. Use it during or after a session to quickly assess where your founder's risks are concentrated.

Risk analysis is the continuous diagnostic that tells you where to spend coaching energy. Don't try to reduce all four risks at once — identify the highest one and focus there. As the founder progresses through milestones, the risk profile shifts. Reassess regularly.
