02 The Coaching Arc
Phase 1
Value Baseline
Weekly, 45-60 min
Intensive
Coach is hands-on — designing protocols, reviewing data, challenging assumptions. This is where you shape the problem hypothesis, run the first experiments, and build the founder's muscle for evidence-based thinking. Highest time investment, highest leverage.
Phase 2
First Customer → X
Bi-weekly, 30 min
Active
Coach shifts from tactical to strategic. Focus moves to growth mechanics, pricing experiments, and early team structure. The founder is executing more independently. Your role: pattern recognition, accountability, and connecting them to the right people.
Phase 3
X → 10X
Bi-weekly to ad-hoc
Loosening
Coach becomes a strategic advisor. Founders are self-directed — they know their metrics, they're running experiments without prompting, they're building teams. You're a sounding board for big decisions, not a weekly driver.
Phase 4
$100K+ → Graduation
Slack-based, ad-hoc
Ad-Hoc
Strategic sessions at major decisions only. Your primary role is as a connector — introductions to investors, advisors, strategic hires, and potential partners. The founder runs the company; you open doors.

Loosening is success, not disengagement. When a founder no longer needs weekly coaching, you've done your job.

03 Two Coaching Models
The Strategic Architect
CEO-to-CEO Level
Focus
Problem definition, positioning, milestone framing, go/no-go decisions
Signature Moves
Foundation Sprint, competitive landscape challenges, network connections
Voice
"What if you framed it this way?"
Best For
Founders who need strategic clarity — they have execution capacity but lack direction or positioning
The Operational Engine
Marketing-Ops Level
Focus
Metrics, channel experiments, tool setup, growth mechanics
Signature Moves
Building alongside founders — analytics dashboards, ad campaigns, landing pages
Voice
"Here, let me build this with you"
Best For
Founders who have direction but need execution support — they know what to do but need help doing it

These are complementary, not interchangeable. The best coaching bench has both models. Know which one you are, and when to bring in the other.

1

The Bottom Line

This methodology isn't theory — it's what we observed across 356 real coaching conversations. The patterns are clear: evidence over opinion, problems before solutions, experiments before features, and accountability in both directions.
