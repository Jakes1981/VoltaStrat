Foundation Sprint

Foundation Sprint

A structured workshop tool that forces clarity on problem, positioning, and execution plan. Not required for every engagement — deploy when a founder needs a strategic reset or when standard coaching isn't producing movement.

01 When to Deploy
1
New Resident Onboarding
A new team that arrives without a clear problem hypothesis or 90-day plan. The Sprint accelerates their starting point — but it's optional, not mandatory. Some teams arrive ready and don't need it.
2
$100K+ Strategic Repositioning
Growth has been execution-driven. Now they need to think strategically: market position, differentiation, scaling plan.
3
Company Is Stuck
Multiple sessions without progress. The founder keeps circling the same issues. A Sprint forces a reset: "Let's go back to first principles."
4
Before a Major Decision
Expansion to a new market, fundraising, pivot, first hire. A Sprint creates the framework for making the decision with evidence.
02 The 7-Step Structure
1
Customer Identification & Segmentation
Map all customer segments. Who are they? How do they differ? Which has the most evidence of pain?
2
Problem Mapping
Apply the Problem Hypothesis template to the primary segment. Who, trigger, problem, impact, root cause, future state.
3
Competitor Analysis & Positioning
Map the competitive landscape. Where are the gaps? What's the founder's unique angle?
4
Capabilities Assessment
What can the team actually do? Technical skills, domain knowledge, distribution access, unfair advantages.
5
Differentiation Mapping
Pick max 4 high-impact dimensions. Plot on X/Y axes. Where does the company clearly win?
6
Values & Principles
What does this company stand for? What trade-offs will they make? This shapes every future decision.
7
Solution Options & Voting
Generate 3–5 solution options based on all the above. Vote. Select. Build the 90-day plan around the winner.
03 Two Formats
1:1 Sprint
Duration2–3 days
ParticipantsCoach + founding team
FormatDeep collaborative work on Miro boards
Best forCompanies at inflection points needing full strategic reset
OutputComplete positioning, ICP, differentiation, 90-day plan
1:Many Sprint
Duration1 day (intensive)
ParticipantsMultiple founding teams simultaneously
FormatSilent Miro collaboration, then group discussion
Best forNew resident onboarding (batch process)
OutputInitial problem hypothesis, risk assessment, first 90-day plan per company
04 Facilitation Tips
Use Miro for everything.
Boards persist after the Sprint. Founders can reference and update them as their understanding evolves.
Silent collaboration first.
Especially in 1:Many — have everyone write simultaneously before discussing. Prevents groupthink.
Timebox each step.
Don't let step 1 eat the whole Sprint. Allocate time proportionally and keep the group moving forward.
Challenge without demoralizing.
Your job is to stress-test, not to validate. Ask hard questions. But frame them as "the market will ask this too."
The output matters more than the process.
A beautiful Miro board that doesn't result in a clear 90-day plan is a failed Sprint.
05 Expected Output
Clear ICP — one specific customer segment, named and characterized
Validated problem hypothesis — filled template, tested against evidence
Differentiated positioning — 4 dimensions, defensible against competition
Risk assessment — 4 risks scored, highest identified
90-day execution plan — specific actions, milestones, experiments

The Foundation Sprint is a powerful tool in the coaching toolkit — not a required step in every engagement. A well-run Sprint can save a founder months of building in the wrong direction. Use it when standard coaching isn't producing movement, when a founder needs a strategic reset, or when a major decision demands structured thinking. For further reading on the sprint methodology, see “Click” by Jake Knapp and John Zeratsky — the framework that inspired this approach.
