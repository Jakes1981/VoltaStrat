## Detailed Assessments

### Revolt App
**Founder:** Khalil Tarabulsi | **Website:** getrevolt.vercel.app | **Status:** Clarification requested

**Problem:** Teens and young adults consuming manipulative social media without understanding how feeds are engineered to exploit emotional triggers.

**Assessment:** Promising problem awareness, but the project history emphasizes the need for stronger evidence on customer urgency and monetization mechanics in a consumer market. The key question was whether the team had evidence that teens or parents would pay. A follow-up clarifying email was drafted. The strategic question raised: would Volta take a bet on the team if a 1:1 Residency seat were available, based on mindset, differentiation, and technical plus sales capability?

**Precedent value:** Consumer apps face a higher bar for customer discovery evidence. "Interesting problem" is not enough — need evidence of willingness to pay.

## Cross-Portfolio Patterns

These patterns emerged across assessments and should inform future screening:

1. **Regulated-domain adjacency** was the most common No-Go reason — especially health, fintech/Bitcoin, and trade compliance.

2. **Broad problem definitions** consistently weakened applications. Narrow ICP, specific pain, and clear wedge are prerequisites.

3. **Founder-market fit and dedicated sales/discovery bandwidth** repeatedly surfaced as critical. Technical novelty alone does not create defensibility.

4. **When an application looks plausibly AI-first and software-native,** the next gating question is: is the team actually doing disciplined customer discovery and commercial learning?

5. **Team composition gaps** (no sales founder, no product founder) correlate with No-Go or Network-only outcomes.

6. **Products that SERVE regulated industries** need careful case-by-case evaluation — they are not automatically regulated themselves.
