What This Role Exists To Do

Volta's community needs two things: excellent relationships (Laura's lane) and excellent events (your lane). Amy Conrad is the events and logistics engine of Volta's builder community — the person who makes every event excellent, every Sprint session run smoothly, and every builder-leader feel supported. Strategy comes from Jaco and Matt. Implementation is a partnership — and this role owns the events side of it completely.

Core Responsibilities
  1. 01
    Events Management

    Plan and execute every community event and Sprint session — venue, logistics, invitations, on-the-day operations, and post-event follow-up. The experience of every person who attends a Volta event is on you.

  2. 02
    Builder Activation

    Design and run the touchpoints that turn passive builders into active community participants. Attendance drives, event programming, and community moments that make people want to show up and stay.

  3. 03
    Sprint Operations

    Manage the full operational layer of every Sprint cohort session — logistics, attendance tracking, coordination between builders and facilitators, and post-session documentation. When Sprint works operationally, more builders graduate and feed the Lab.

  4. 04
    Builder-Leader Support

    Volta's builder-leaders run the community at the local level across Atlantic Canada. You are their operational support — helping them run their events, access resources, and stay connected to Volta's programmes. Responsive and reliable is the standard.

  5. 05
    Community Data Collection

    Track attendance, engagement, and event outcomes across all community activities. This data feeds directly into Volta's community health systems — it's what makes the whole reporting picture work for the team.

The Day-to-Day

Most of your time is spent on events, activations, and builder relationships — the on-the-ground work that makes the community real. There's an operational and admin layer too: coordination, follow-through, and data processing that comes with running regular programming at this scale.

What We're Looking For
  • An operator — you follow through on everything, every time, with no dropped balls
  • An energetic presence — builders feel your energy and it draws them in, makes them want to come back
  • Detail-oriented — logistics, comms, and data all get handled with precision
  • Relationship-driven — you build genuine connections with the builders you support and the BLs you serve
  • Adaptable — community work is dynamic and sometimes chaotic, and you handle it without drama
Performance Indicators
Q2 Leading Indicators
  • Event attendance: active builders at ≥1 event per month
  • Sprint session logistics: zero operational failures per cohort
  • Builder activation rate: passive → active conversion improving
  • Data collection: complete and on-time for every event
  • BL responsiveness: <24h turnaround on builder-leader requests
Lagging Indicators · Assessed July 1
  • Builder retention: % still engaged 3 months after first event
  • Sprint cohort satisfaction: builder-reported scores
  • Event reach: total unique builders engaged per quarter
  • BL health: self-reported support satisfaction from builder-leaders
