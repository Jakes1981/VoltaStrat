How the Three Pillars Connect
Builders & Community → SME AI Lab
The community is the Lab's talent source
The SME Lab places vetted AI builders with local businesses to solve real problems. Those vetted builders come from the community Laura runs. Without a healthy, active builder network, the Lab has no one to deploy — so Laura's community work directly enables the Lab's revenue targets.
Sprint Program → Startup Success
Builders can become startup co-founders
Sprint is a structured 40-hour development program (2 hours/week) that turns community builders into skilled, vetted AI practitioners. The best of those graduates don't just do Lab engagements — the strongest can be matched as technical co-founders for portfolio startups that need AI capability on the founding team.
Community Health → Program Design
What Laura sees informs how Volta runs
Laura's monthly community health synthesis — engagement data, builder feedback, Pulse scores, event patterns — gives Jaco and Matt a ground-level view that portfolio numbers alone can't show. These insights shape how the Residency is structured, how coaches are briefed, and where Volta invests its energy next.

Day-to-Day Admin & Data Hygiene
The recurring operational work — this is the 25–30%. AI assists, but Laura owns the judgment calls.
1
Residency applications
Review and process new applications, manage comms for interviews, handle post-interview follow-up, and move applicants through the application and review process to a decision.
2
Resident onboarding & access
Onboard new residents into the program. Ensure every new and existing resident knows what they have access to, where to find it, and how to use it — tools, resources, coaching, community.
3
AWS application approvals
Review and process incoming AWS credit applications for portfolio companies. Routine but time-sensitive.
4
Support engagement hours & invoice reconciliation
Review all open support engagements and liaise with advisors and coaches to ensure hours approved in Impact OS align with hours logged. When invoices are submitted, verify they tie to what's in the system before they move to finance.
5
Company update monitoring
Monitor company updates in Impact OS for all residents — with extra attention on those in 1:1 coaching support. Regular updates are the data foundation that makes coaching and evaluations possible.
6
Pre-evaluation monthly updates
Companies waiting for a 1:1 coaching seat need a monthly update submitted before evaluation week (the last week of every month). Laura ensures these are in on time so nothing falls through the cracks at evaluation.
7
Monthly evaluation processing & coach allocation
Work with Jaco each month to process evaluations — reviewing which teams are ready for 1:1 support, which need to wait, and which may not continue. Once decided, allocate teams to the right coaches for the coming month.

Volta's Agent Automation Stack
Volta has built a full agent automation stack that will be made available to you. It's designed to take the repetitive coordination and synthesis work off your plate — so your time stays where it matters most.
Communications
Inbox triage, email drafting, and follow-up sequencing — so you're never starting from scratch on routine comms. Draft a coach briefing, a founder update, or a community announcement in seconds.
Scheduling & Prep
Calendar management and pre-meeting briefing docs generated automatically. You walk into every coaching session, team meeting, or founder check-in already prepared with the context you need.
Daily Briefing
A morning summary of what matters — portfolio flags, priority actions, community updates. You start each day knowing where to focus, without having to dig through systems to find it.
Meeting Intelligence
Coaching sessions and team meetings are automatically transcribed and synthesised. Action items extracted, follow-ups flagged. No manual note-taking, no lost decisions.
Data Queries
Ask questions about the portfolio in plain language and get answers directly from Impact OS. "Which companies haven't submitted an update this month?" "What's the current coaching allocation?" — answered instantly.
Reporting & Synthesis
Community health reports, portfolio snapshots, and monthly summaries can be drafted by the stack and reviewed by you — rather than built from scratch. Your job is judgment, not assembly.
The bottom line: This role would be 60–70% admin at most organisations. At Volta, the agent stack absorbs that overhead. You get access to a system that handles the repetitive so you can focus on the relational — the founder conversations, the coach relationships, the community health, and the strategic decisions that actually move things forward.
